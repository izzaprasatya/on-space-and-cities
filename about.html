About Me

A half-boiled geographer and an urbanist. Loves coffee and doing spatial analysis casually.

Location

South Jakarta, DKI Jakarta
Indonesia

At first, I wanted to call myself a hard-boiled geographer, inspired by the fictional character Philip Marlowe—the hard-boiled detective created by Raymond Chandler in The Long Goodbye. Yet I soon realized that such a label felt premature. My geographical capacity is still something to be explored and deepened. I have a long journey ahead before I could ever become a hard-boiled geographer. This realization made me aware that, for now, I am still a half-boiled geographer, one in the process of becoming.

I earned a Bachelor of Arts in Geography, with a specialization in physical geography, focusing on geomorphology, hydrology, and climatology within the context of urban studies. Throughout my studies, I also developed a strong interest in urban geography, economic geography, and cultural geography. In my spare time, I enjoy writing reflective pieces on urban life and the quiet contemplations of the city.
